MacRitchie Reservoir Park

The Location

If you're looking for a nice chill place for jogging, canoe or just to detach from civilization and have a walk, MacRitchie Reservoir Park could be the place you are looking for.
The park has lighting from 7 pm to 7 am, has many nearby bus stops and is a stone's throw away from Caldecott MRT.
They have facilities in place for your enjoyment and convenience including a carpark, pathway for jogging and walking, large bodies of water for canoe as well as places to take pictures.

The Trail

MacRitchie has several pathways which many use for jogging or walking. The trail is roughly 11 km, and winds around the reservoir as well as wildlife in the area. Individuals can also choose hike in the 3.2 km and 4.8 km trails around the more forested regions of the park.

The Reservoir

MacRitchie is a place where everyone can rent Kayaks and Canoes at the Paddle Lodge. There are designated areas for first timers and more experienced paddlers.

The Scenery

Below you can see some images that others have took at the reservoir. Individuals have also taken pictures of the birds and other floral and fauna.
The reservoir park has a suspension bridge called the TreeTop Walk which connects the 2 highest points in the reservoir, giving park visiters a fantastic view.
Birdwatching and Photography are common activities some partake in.

Do's and Don'ts

According to the website made by National Parks, there are several rules and advisories to follow when in the park. Some of them include but are not limited to:

  • Do not make excessive amounts of noise
  • Clean up after your pets
  • No Smoking
  • No litering
  • Stay on the appropriate trails
  • Do not disturb the wildlife, this includes:
    • Do not feed the animals
    • Do not release pets into the park
    • Do not bring your pets in the vicnity of the wildlife habitats.
